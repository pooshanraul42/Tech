3D Piping & Equipment Layout – Solvent Recovery Plant

Designed using SolidWorks, this project illustrates equipment placement, piping routes, and mechanical integration for an industrial solvent recovery process.

The project involved designing a solvent recovery plant layout within a site-defined footprint using GA drawings and P&ID as inputs. Emphasis was placed on space optimization, process flow integrity, and manufacturability. All piping was routed with elevation control and ergonomics in mind, and output included a complete assembly and BOM. The system followed ASME piping norms and industry-standard practices for fabrication and erection feasibility.

Complete Plant Assembly Overview

The 3D layout includes process vessels such as a reactor, distillation column, condensers, and storage tanks. All components were arranged for minimal footprint, logical flow, and future scalability.

  • Equipment: 20 KL Reactor, 10M Column, 30m² & 10m² Condensers
  • Process Units: 200L Reflux Drum, 1.5 KL Receiver
  • Outcome: Fabrication-ready model with full BOM

Dimetric Routing Validation

Dimetric projection ensured elevation-based clash checks between service lines. All routing was performed using P&ID-driven logic, grouped by process zone.

  • Elevation Levels: Multi-floor layout with 3000mm spacing
  • Validation: Cross-checked with plant GA and structure
  • Result: No interference between lines or platforms

Boiler and Cooling Zone Design

Thermal equipment such as chillers, thermic fluid heaters, and oil tanks were integrated based on OEM interface details and layout specifications.

  • Drainage Floor: 25×4 mm strip grating for fluid removal
  • Ventilation Zones: Allocated for all heat-generating equipment
  • OEM Considerations: Pipe flange orientation, support brackets

Manufacturing-Ready Condenser Design

Condensers were modeled with fabrication details including nozzle pads, shell specs, and weld interfaces to comply with ASME fabrication drawings.

  • Standard: ASME Section VIII layout principles
  • Details Included: Nozzle flanges, dish heads, supports
  • Ready for: Fabrication drawing, BOM extraction

P&ID-Driven Piping Logic

P&ID documents were used to assign line numbers, sizes, pipe schedules, and valve types. Threaded (NPT) connections were reserved strictly for instrumentation.

  • Valves Used: Ball, Gate, Globe, NRVs, Control valves
  • Pipe Grouping: Process, pneumatic, utility, instrumentation
  • Output: Final tagged BOM with unique identifiers

*Note: P&ID files are protected under NDA and not shown here.

Project Summary & Layout Workflow

During my internship, I developed a full 3D layout of a solvent recovery plant based on client-supplied GA drawings and P&ID. The design accounted for equipment spacing, piping feasibility, and site constraints, ensuring manufacturability and clean routing.

GA & P&ID Interpretation → Equipment Modeling in SolidWorks → Structural Platform Creation → Piping Routing → Dimetric View Validation → BOM Extraction & Feasibility Check

The final layout reduced material overlap, optimized pipe lengths, and enabled smooth on-site installation with minimal adjustments.
