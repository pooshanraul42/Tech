Clarifier Walkway Design for Wastewater Treatment Plant

Structural design and FEA validation of a clarifier walkway bridge.

This project involved the structural design, finite element analysis, and installation planning of a clarifier scraper bridge for a wastewater treatment plant. The 11-meter truss was engineered to support up to 1000 kg, accounting for the gearbox, drive pipe, scraper arm, and maintenance loads. Key objectives included lightweight design, bolt-together assembly for transport, and structural integrity under service conditions.

Clarifier Walkway Assembly

The structure was built using 50×50×5 mm L-angle trusses and designed in SolidWorks with trim-extended joints.

  • Split into two segments using bolted joints for transport and on-site assembly
  • Diagonal and horizontal bracing added to reduce torsional deformation
  • Mounting features modeled for gearbox, scraper pipe

Integration with Civil Infrastructure

The truss was designed within a cylindrical concrete tank envelope to ensure proper fit and clearance.

  • Anchoring plates aligned to match feedwell and top concrete rim
  • Scraper shaft and couplings referenced per site layout
  • Cross-checked against P&ID and plant layout drawings

Mesh & Boundary Setup

Finite element modeling was performed in ANSYS Mechanical using structured hex elements.

  • Shell and beam elements used to balance fidelity and solve time
  • Mesh refinement applied at bolt holes, load-bearing brackets, and gusseted corners
  • Mesh metrics like aspect ratio, skewness, and warping were checked

Load Case

The truss was analyzed for a conservative 1000 kg load to account for motor torque and scraper resistance.

  • 1000 kg point load applied at gearbox location (center span)
  • Self-weight, equipment mass, and maintenance live load included
  • Boundary conditions: gravity + fixed supports at anchor plates

Stress Analysis

Stress concentrations were examined to ensure structural safety across all load paths.

  • Peak Von Mises stress: ~153 MPa near bolt cutouts and joint overlaps
  • Stresses remained well below the steel yield strength (250 MPa)
  • Stress mitigated using bracing plates and filleted welds

Deflection Check

The deformation was plotted and compared against deflection limits for walkable structures.

  • Max vertical deflection: ~5 mm (L/220 approx.)
  • Within the L/250 limit for platforms under static loads
  • Deflection uniform along mid-span with no torsional twist

Factor of Safety

  • Minimum FOS > 2.3 under worst-case loading
  • FOS validated at all bolted junctions and load paths
  • Redundant structure supports future load variations

Manufacturing Drawing

Detailed GA drawing was prepared for fabrication and inspection:

  • Included BOM, welding specifications, and fit tolerances
  • Assembly split defined for site handling
  • Issued to vendor after design verification

*Note: Drawings are protected under NDA and not shown here.

Conclusion

The clarifier bridge was structurally verified for load-bearing capacity and site compatibility. Following analysis and drawing release, it was fabricated and installed on-site with no reported issues during commissioning.

Project Workflow Summary

This project followed a full-cycle design process: CAD modeling, structural simulation, and fabrication readiness. All load cases were verified through FEA and drawings were finalized based on plant feedback.

CAD in SolidWorks → Tank Layout Coordination → FEA in ANSYS → Stress & Deflection Validation → GA Drawing Release
