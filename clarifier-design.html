Clarifier Walkway Assembly

This project involved the structural design and simulation of a clarifier scraper walkway mechanism. The CAD model was developed to suit integration within the tank and evaluated under service loads using FEA.

Tank Interface & Layout

The walkway was modeled in the context of the clarifier tank envelope to ensure proper clearance and installation alignment. It shows structural coordination with civil concrete elements.

FEA Setup & Mesh Refinement

The walkway truss was meshed using beam and shell elements. Additional refinement was applied at the base connection and load-bearing members to increase convergence accuracy.

Load Cases

Service loads included self-weight and maintenance live load. Constraints were applied to match real-world fixed base plate anchorage conditions.

Stress Analysis

Von Mises stress distribution indicated maximum values near welded joints and bracing cutouts. These values remained under material yield limits, confirming safe design.

Deformation Behavior

Maximum deformation under service load was within acceptable deflection limits (L/250), ensuring user safety and platform stability.

Factor of Safety

FOS contours highlight reinforcement regions. The minimum safety factor was greater than 2 across all members under static loading conditions.

Manufacturing Drawing

The final GA drawing includes member schedules, weld notes, and fabrication tolerances in a detailed production-ready format.

Conclusion

This clarifier walkway project covered 3D modeling, civil coordination, and structural verification through FEA. The final assembly drawing was handed off for fabrication and installation on-site.
