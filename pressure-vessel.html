Pressure Vessel Design & FEA

Stress analysis and validation of a 500L vessel with nozzle loads and thermal pressure, verified using ANSYS per ASME Section VIII Div. 2.

This project involved the design and validation of a 500L pressure vessel subjected to 3 kg/cm² internal pressure and 150°C operating temperature. The analysis was conducted using ANSYS Mechanical, following ASME Section VIII Div. 2 rules. Loads included internal pressure, nozzle thrusts and moments, self-weight, and thermal expansion. Results were validated through stress linearization and compared against ASME allowable limits.

3D CAD Model

The vessel was modeled in SolidWorks with a cylindrical shell, torispherical dish ends, and six nozzle connections. Geometric simplifications such as fillets and chamfers were excluded to ensure a mesh-friendly model for FEA.

  • Volume: 500L | Thickness: 6 mm
  • Nozzles: Handhole, Vent, Drain, Vacuum, Spare, View Glass
  • Dish Ends: 10% Torispherical

Mesh Strategy

A multizone mesh with sweep method was applied to sweepable bodies. Mesh refinement was provided near nozzle junctions with at least two elements across thickness for accuracy.

  • Element Type: SOLID186 (Quadratic, 20-node)
  • Control: Local edge sizing & method controls
  • Validation: Skewness, aspect ratio, and Jacobian

Loading & Boundary Conditions

Internal pressure of 0.2942 MPa was applied along with self-weight and nozzle loads as per WRC guidelines. Nozzle ends were constrained with radial and bending moments.

  • Supports: Fixed base and flanges
  • Loads: Pressure, nozzle thrusts, gravity
  • Moments: Applied on cylindrical and spherical shells

Stress Results

Von Mises stress was highest at the nozzle-shell intersections. Stress values were below yield limits for SA-516 Gr.70 and showed safe margins for pressure containment.

  • Max Stress: 159.85 MPa
  • Material Allowable: 235.5 MPa (1.5 × Sm)

Total Deformation

Deformation primarily occurred at unsupported nozzles and dish crown. Maximum deflection recorded was under 1.2 mm, verifying structural rigidity.

  • Peak Deflection: 1.14 mm
  • Location: At nozzle neck edges
Stress linearization result with tabular breakdown across shell thickness.

Stress Linearization

A classification line was plotted across shell thickness at the nozzle intersection. Membrane and bending stress components were extracted and compared against allowable limits as per ASME Div. 2 Appendix 5.

  • Max Linearized Stress (Pl + Pb): 119.74 MPa
  • Allowable: 1.5 × Sm = 235.5 MPa
  • Result: ✔ Pass

Project Summary & Analysis Workflow

As part of my bachelor's thesis, I modeled and analyzed a pressure vessel subject to internal pressure, temperature, and nozzle loading. The vessel was validated as per ASME Section VIII Division 2 using linearized stress components and comparison against allowable stress criteria.

External nozzle loadings were interpreted using a WRC bulletin (2002 edition), accounting for axial, bending, and torsional components. These inputs enabled a realistic simulation of service-level stress behavior at nozzle-shell junctions.

CAD Modeling in SolidWorks → Geometry Cleanup → Import to ANSYS Workbench → Mesh Refinement → Load Application → Stress Linearization → ASME Code Validation → Experimental Hydrotesting

Material Validation: Properties were selected from ASME Section II, Part D, with yield strength values at temperature referenced from Table Y-1. Allowable stress limits were determined per Annex 3-A, ensuring conformance with plastic collapse criteria under Division 2 rules.

This approach confirmed structural feasibility and demonstrated proficiency in both pressure vessel design standards and simulation workflow.
