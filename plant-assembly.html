3D Piping & Equipment Layout – Solvent Recovery Plant

Designed using SolidWorks, this project illustrates equipment placement, piping routes, and mechanical integration for an industrial solvent recovery process.

The project involved developing a modular equipment layout for a solvent recovery plant. Emphasis was placed on accessibility, equipment spacing, and clean routing of process pipelines. Core design principles included adherence to space management, equipment orientation, and feasibility for manufacturing, installation and maintenance. All models were created using SolidWorks.

Complete Plant Assembly Overview

Full modular layout including reactor, distillation column, condensers, utility tanks, and interconnecting piping. Equipment was arranged to optimize flow, minimize interference, and support ease of maintenance within the client-defined footprint.

  • Client-constrained site layout served as the spatial boundary.
  • Clearances maintained for operator access and maintenance tools.
  • Layout planned for future expansion without repositioning core units.

Dimetric Routing Validation

Dimetric projection helped validate 3D pipe runs, elevation levels, and interconnections. Routing was optimized using P&ID line codes and elevation planning to reduce material use and ensure assembly feasibility.

  • Reduced piping length through elevation-aware layout.
  • Avoided overlaps between process and utility pipelines.
  • Elevation strategy ensured feasible support placements.

Boiler and Cooling Zone Design

Thermal and utility systems such as chillers and thermic fluid heaters were arranged to maintain ventilation and service access. Floor spacing (3000 mm) allowed ergonomic operation and future modifications. Open slotted plates (25×4 mm) ensured drainage in case of leaks.

  • Component spacing ensures safety during thermal load fluctuations.
  • Designed walkable maintenance zones around each utility.
  • Equipment positioned to minimize pipe length and joint count.

Manufacturing-Ready Condenser Design

These vessels were modeled with fabrication-ready detail including flanges, weld zones, nozzle specs, and internal baffles. Designed using ASME guidelines, they are suitable for production and documentation purposes.

  • Included custom flange types and port configurations.
  • Modeled from GA drawing inputs to exact scale and tolerances.
  • Ready-to-use CAD data for shop-level documentation.

P&ID-Driven Piping Logic

The P&ID governed pipe sizing, line codes, flow directions, and instrumentation. Routing followed material specs and valve placements indicated in the P&ID, with flanged connections for process lines and NPT for instrumentation.

  • Valve types included: Ball, Globe, Gate, NRV, Butterfly.
  • Piping grouped by service type: process, pneumatics, instrumentation.
  • Final routing matched 1:1 with flow sequencing on P&ID.
