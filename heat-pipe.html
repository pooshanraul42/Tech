Modelling of Novel Heat Pipe Design using ANSYS Fluent

This is validation of a Novel heat pipe concept for fusion relevant condition using ANSYS Fluent.

This project investigates the thermal behavior of a molybdenum-lithium heat pipe under fusion-relevant heat loads using CFD. A reduced-order axisymmetric model was developed in ANSYS Fluent and benchmarked against COMSOL simulations from TU/e. The goal was to simulate evaporation condensation cycle and identify heat flux performance in a simplified domain.

Novel Heat Pipe

The heat pipe is essentially made form 3D printed molybdenum and has internal capillaries lined with lithium. This structure is designed to sustain heat fluxes upto 20MW/m2, resembling to conditions in nuclear fusion.

  • The pipe works on evaporation condensation cycle of liquid lithium
  • The pipe are vacuum sealed for preventing any oxidation
  • This heat pipe construction is capable of sustaining upto 2000Â°C

Working Principle

Heat transfer occurs through lithium vaporization and condensation along the axial length of the pipe.

  • Vaporization at hot end: ~1600 K peak under high heat flux
  • Condensation at the cooler region due to capillary action
  • No external pumping or active flow required

Problem Idealization

The domain was simplified to 2D axissymmetric model to reduce element count and improve computational efficiency for this complex solution.

  • Solid, liquid, and vapour zones defined explicitly
  • Mesh controls were applied to for generating conformal mesh
  • VOF model was used to represent liquid vapour interaction

Mesh Refinement & Quality

Quadrilateral mesh was applied with sizing controls near steep gradients for better numerical stability.

  • Refined evaporator zone with 0.4 mm spacing
  • Skewness ~0.32, orthogonality ~0.74
  • Reduced interpolation errors at wall interfaces

Mesh Metrics

Mesh quality was evaluated using standard Fluent checks before solver initialization.

  • Aspect ratio < 3 across entire domain
  • Gradient smoothing applied post-refinement
  • Zero negative volumes or element collapse

Mesh Convergence Study

Mesh convergence was achieved at ~38,000 nodes & ~11400 elements with less than 2% deviation in peak temperature.

  • Solver time reduced by ~15% vs baseline
  • Grid convergence index within acceptable tolerance
  • Final mesh used for all thermal post-processing

Temperature Results

Peak surface temperatures (~1600 K) matched reference COMSOL data from TU Eindhoven.

  • Localized peaks at evaporator edge and mid-wick
  • Stable axial drop toward condenser confirmed
  • Temperature tracking over 12.3 s of transient simulation

Cross-Validation

Temperature field matched benchmark thermal data despite simplified phase boundaries.

  • Minor deviation near liquid-vapor interface
  • Axial and radial distribution trends consistent
  • Validated without full VOF model implementation

Conclusion

This study validates reduced-order CFD modeling for fusion-grade heat pipe performance. Future work includes multiphase VOF refinement, UDF-based flux inputs, and electromagnetic coupling via MHD modules.

Project Summary & CFD Workflow

A novel heat pipe geometry was simulated for use in fusion applications. Axisymmetric simplification reduced computational demand while retaining heat transfer accuracy. The Fluent model was validated against benchmark COMSOL results, demonstrating good thermal correlation despite phase simplification.
