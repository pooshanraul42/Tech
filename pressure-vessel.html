Pressure Vessel Design & FEA

This project involved designing and analyzing a pressurised tank with 10% torispherical dishends under internal pressure, in accordance with ASME BPVC Section VIII Division 2 guidelines. The vessel was evaluated for structural safety under operational loads using ANSYS Mechanical.

3D CAD Model

The vessel includes a cylindrical shell, hemispherical heads, and multiple nozzle openings. Modeled in SolidWorks, exported as STEP for meshing and simulation.

Mesh Strategy

A tetrahedral mesh with refinement around nozzle junctions and fillets was applied. The global element size was set to X mm with inflation layers at pressure-facing surfaces.

Loading & Boundary Conditions

The vessel was subjected to internal pressure of XX MPa with fixed support constraints at nozzle flanges. Radial loads applied on nozzles to simulate piping reactions.

Stress Results

Von Mises stress distribution highlights critical regions at nozzle-shell junctions. Peak stress remains below allowable limits for SA-516 Gr.70 material.

Total Deformation

Maximum deflection observed at unsupported nozzle ends is under 1.2 mm. Structural integrity and stiffness are confirmed.

Stress Linearization

Linearized stresses across a critical path show membrane + bending components. Compared with allowable limits per ASME Appendix 5, confirming safe operation.
