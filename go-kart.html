Racing Go-Kart – Structural Design & Simulation

Designed for national-level competitions, this project focuses on strength, safety, and performance of a race-ready go-kart chassis through FEA-based validation.

Designed and validated a competitive go-kart chassis for national-level go-karting events. The goal was to create a safe, performance-oriented vehicle optimized for strength, weight, and manufacturability. SolidWorks was used for modeling and ANSYS for crash and modal simulations.

Final Assembly Render

Complete CAD model including chassis, engine, steering, and body frame. Designed to meet rules and dynamic handling performance.

  • Engine: 124.6 cc | Max Speed: 80 km/h
  • Chassis Weight: 9.2 kg
  • Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS

Chassis CAD Model

The chassis was modeled using AISI 4130 tubular steel for its high strength-to-weight ratio. The structure was optimized for rigidity and impact absorption.

  • Tube Spec: 29.2 mm OD × 1.65 mm thick
  • Material: AISI 4130 | Yield Strength: 768 MPa
  • Focus: Safety-compliant and lightweight design

Mesh Overview

Tetrahedral meshing was applied with refinement at high-stress zones including welds and suspension nodes. Element quality checks ensured accurate results.

  • Refinement: Engine + steering mount areas
  • Validation: Mesh convergence confirmed

Front Impact Analysis

A 4G deceleration load (5886 N) was applied to the front chassis. The design was reinforced post-analysis for improved safety.

  • Max Stress: 301.56 MPa | FOS: 2.19
  • Deformation: 0.967 mm

Rear Impact Analysis

A rear-end collision at 2.5G (3678 N) was simulated. Frame stiffness was retained and bracing validated for engine protection.

  • Max Stress: 245.4 MPa | FOS: 2.69
  • Deformation: 2.15 mm

Torsional Rigidity

Applied torque of 367.8 Nm to simulate cornering load. Deflection and twist were well within safe tolerances.

  • Deformation: 6.13 mm
  • Max Stress: 613.98 MPa

Modal Frequency Analysis

Natural frequencies were analyzed to ensure separation from engine RPM (75Hz). Modes at 59Hz and 117Hz validated safe operation.

  • Primary Modes: 59 Hz and 117 Hz
  • Safe Margin: No structural resonance

Project Summary & Engineering Workflow

This project focused on designing a structurally optimized go-kart chassis for competitive use. The design was validated against impact, torsional, and modal conditions using finite element analysis. Key priorities included driver safety, frame stiffness, and manufacturability using standard tubing and welding processes.

Concept Sketch → CAD Modeling in SolidWorks → Material Selection → FEA Setup in ANSYS → Impact & Modal Simulations → Design Iteration & Validation

The validated frame design met all safety and performance targets, with clear documentation of stress zones and structural limits under race-like conditions.
